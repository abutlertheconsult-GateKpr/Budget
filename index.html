Most $ Saved
⛰️ Debt Avalanche
Pay minimums on all debts, then throw every extra dollar at the highest APR first. Mathematically optimal — saves the most interest over time.
Best: Interest savingsNeeds discipline
Learn more at Investopedia →
Most Motivating
❄️ Debt Snowball
Target the lowest balance first. Each payoff creates momentum. Made famous by Dave Ramsey.
Best: Quick winsCosts slightly more
Learn more at Investopedia →
⚖️ Equal Distribution
Split extra payments equally across all debts. Balanced approach, reduces all balances simultaneously.
CFPB Debt Guide →
🔗 Balance Transfer
Move high-APR credit card debt to a 0% intro APR card. Pay aggressively during the promo period (usually 12–21 months). Watch for 3–5% transfer fees.
Compare Balance Transfer Cards →
🤝 Debt Management Plan
A nonprofit credit counselor negotiates lower rates for you. One monthly payment distributed to creditors. Doesn't hurt credit like settlement does.
Find an NFCC Counselor →
